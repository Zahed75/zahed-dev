The Narrative

Scaling Engineering
Without Chaos.

What works for a team of 5 engineers catastrophically fails at 50. Communication paths multiply exponentially, and without intentional architectural and organizational design, delivery velocity grinds to a halt.

Throughout my career, I've observed this pattern repeatedly. Founders sprint to product-market fit, accumulating massive technical and organizational debt. When the time comes to scale the company, the monolithic architecture and siloed engineering teams become the primary bottleneck to growth.

At Syscomatic LLC, we solve exactly this. We implement Software Delivery as a Service (SDaaS)—deploying high-functioning, cross-functional engineering pods that integrate seamlessly into enterprise roadmaps. My role as Engineering Manager is to architect both the systems we deploy, and the teams that build them.

"Technical debt is a tool, not a sin. The failure is not in accumulating it, but in failing to schedule its amortization."

I specialize in Distributed Systems Architecture (Python, Go, Kubernetes), Team Topology Design (Spotify Model, Domain-Driven Squads), and establishing high-trust, low-friction engineering cultures.
